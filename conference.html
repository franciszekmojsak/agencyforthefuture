design business conference

A new series of conferences for architects, designers, artists, and innovators

Design Business is a series dedicated to the key directions of contemporary design and architecture - from ethical and sustainable development, through innovative technologies, to the social role of design. The idea behind is led by Wise Habit and ArchiSnob — an organization supporting designers and architects in creating conscious, responsible solutions where business is a core of every decision.

The creators of the initiative believe that meaningful dialogue shapes the future of the industry, which is why they invite representatives of architecture, design, science, business, and NGOs to join the conversation. We meet in Warsaw, Poland.

  • How to build ethical and profitable business around design?
  • How to earn and stay fair. Building longterm potential based on values and consumer involvement?
  • How to design ethically, consciously and with the future in mind?
  • How do we connect: business with branding, design with efficient implementation, technology with craftsmanship, innovation with care for the planet?

about the organizers

Wise Habit & ArchiSnob

Wise Habit

Wise Habit is a strategic design agency committed to shaping a better future. We create conferences and exhibitions that inspire on an intellectual rather than purely aesthetic level. Focusing on ethically relevant topics, we use design and architecture as tools to provoke thought, encourage dialogue, and contribute to meaningful change.

ArchiSnob

ArchiSnob is a platform dedicated to shaping the dialogue around architecture and design through critical thinking, cultural insight, and real industry impact. Founded and led by Marcin Szczelina, it brings together expertise in architecture, design, and sustainability to inspire meaningful conversations at conferences and industry events worldwide.

Why?

The Design & Business conference exists to connect design with real-world impact. Not as theory, but as practice. It responds to a simple need: design today must work — economically, socially, environmentally, and ethically. Ideas are not enough. They need systems, strategy, and execution. The conference explores how creativity becomes value: how brands are built, how products reach the market, and how decisions in design shape broader business outcomes.

To whom?

The conference is designed to build a strong and engaged community by facilitating knowledge exchange and fostering collaboration across diverse audience groups.

  • Architects, Contract Companies and Developers
  • Product Designers
  • Manufacturers
  • Buyers

accompanying events

Ethical Design Exhibition & ArchiSnob Gala

Wise Habit Exhibition: The Power of Three is an exhibition that approaches design and architecture as a tool for reflection on the contemporary world. Its structure is inspired by the number three — an archetypal symbol of balance, cyclicality, and harmony. The exhibition unfolds across three thematic axes: Sustainability, Ethical Design, and Crisis, forming a framework for dialogue about the future of the built environment.

Each new issue of ArchiSnob is celebrated with a special evening event — a natural continuation of the conversations started during the day. Following the Design & Business conference, guests are invited to stay for the official premiere and evening gala. The event brings together architects, curators, investors, and opinion leaders — attended by over 400 guests from leading Polish design studios. Each edition is accompanied by a concert by a renowned Polish artist or a specially created performance.

venue

The National Library of Poland, Warsaw

The events of the day will take place at the National Library of Poland in Warsaw — one of the country's most important cultural institutions, founded in 1928. The main building, designed in the 1960s by architect Stanisław Fijałkowski, is a landmark of Polish modernism. Recently modernized by KONIOR Studio, the library has been carefully transformed to meet contemporary needs while preserving its original architectural character.
